Background

  • Historically, CO2 emissions have been primarily driven by increasing fuel consumption, which has, and continues to be, a fundamental pillar of economic growth. However, the relationship between CO2 emissions and economic growth has been well established. There might be some more specific detailed factors that drive GDP growth and in the meantime influence CO2 emission.

  • For these detailed factors, I am looking at access to electricity, urban population, rural population, agricultural industry value, investment in environment related industries such as water, energy and transportation and so on. These factors are related to economic growth and also affect the environment.

  • The purpose of this analysis is to see among these variables I collected what might be the real important economical variable that is driving CO2 emissions.

Methods considered and used and rationale

  • Data Wrangling

    The raw data collected for this project are dirty and have different forms. Also, there are several datasets that need to be combined. So functions like select, gather, arrange, bind_cols are used.

  • Data Visualization

    When it comes to the overview of variables before doing anything abd which model fits the best and variable importance, graphs are more straightforward.

  • Machine Learning (regression) (variable importance)

    Since the goal is to explore the relationship between outcome variable (CO2 emission) and predictors, regression is necessary. In addition, it is important to know which variable is most important in this process.
