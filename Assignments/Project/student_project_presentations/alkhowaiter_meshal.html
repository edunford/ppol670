Background

What is my data?

I will be using administrative individual-level data that I have acquired for my thesis, from the Ministry of Labor and Social Development in Saudi Arabia. The dataset is for the Teachers Wage Subsidy program, which is a program that was introduced in 2012 to absorb and employ the large number of unemployed teachers at the time.

How the program works?

The Teachers Wage Subsidy program schema requires subsidized workers in the education sector to find a job at a private school that is willing to hire them and that has agreed to participate in the program. The program offers a 50% wage subsidy, and the subsidy lasts for five years.

The program obliges participating private schools to pay a minimum of 5600 Riyals per month, but schools have the option to pay their subsidized teachers more if they decide to.

The subsidy period is five years, unless:

A teahcer leaves the program for another higher paying private sector job.

Problem Statement

What is the objective of this project?

I want to predict which individuals are more likely to leave welfare and find an unsubsidized private sector job, using approximately:

20 individual level variables or characteristics that include an individual's:

level of education, region, city, age, school size, years of experience, and type (i.e. international, traditional, versus modern schools), gender, wage, and job title.

Policy Relevance

The policy relevance of this is to formulate an understanding of what factors and characteristics cause a welfare recipient to stay or drop out of the program, in Saudi Arabia. For instance, one insightful finding may be that a schools geographic location, is a better predictor of job retention for a subsidized worker in the education sector, than factors such as wage and gender, which policy analysts may perceive as are more relevant in Saudi Arabias context.

What Methods Have Been Considered?
